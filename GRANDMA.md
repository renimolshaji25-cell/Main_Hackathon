A Note for Grandma
The Problem: "The Digital Pickpocket"
Grandma, you know how we always keep our purses close in a crowded market? The internet is the same way. Sometimes, people send messages pretending to be the electric company or the bank, saying you owe money or won a prize. They aren't trying to help; they are "digital pickpockets" trying to sneak into your accounts and take the money you've worked so hard to save.

What my "Safe-Click" does: "Your Personal Neighborhood Watch"
I built this tool to be like a trusted friend who walks with you to the market. Before you open a suspicious letter or click a link on your phone, you show it to this tool first. It’s like having a Neighborhood Watch captain right on your screen.

How it helps you stay safe:

It spots the "Fake ID": Just like someone wearing a fake uniform, a bad link might look real but have one letter wrong. My tool sees those tiny mistakes that are hard for our eyes to catch.

It talks to you plainly: It won't use fancy computer words. It will say, "Wait, Grandma, this person is acting too hurried and pushy. That's a sign they are trying to trick you."

It listens and remembers: If you’re confused, you can ask, "Are you sure?" or "What should I do next?" It remembers what you just asked, so you don't have to repeat yourself.

The Goal:
I want you to be able to use your phone to talk to family and read the news without worrying that someone is going to take your grocery money. This is my way of looking out for you, even when I'm not sitting right next to you.
